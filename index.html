CODE

I've been programming since about middle school, starting with Scratch and Python before learning more advanced languages.

I became particularly interested in graphics programming and learned systems languages like C++ and Rust, as well as GPU APIs like OpenGL and WGPU for Rust. I really like working with (and against) these libraries to make fast, efficient, and visual programs.

Most of all, I love creating. If one of my creations is able to help solve a problem or make someone happy, I'm satisfied.

WORK

ARL:UT

Applied Research Laboratories at the University of Texas Austin J.J. Pickle Center

Apprentice and Research Assistant

ARL:UT is a University-Affiliated Research Center (UARC) partnered with the University of Texas at Austin. It recieves funding from and contracts with the US Navy. I worked in the Signal and Informational Sciences Laboratory (SISL) in the Geospatial Laser Applications & Measurements (GLAM) group with NASA.
We focused on glacial LiDAR imaging, data visualization, database programming, and fluid dynamics. By the end of my apprenticeship, my partner and I wrote a paper about calculating the turbulence of a glacial plume from its satellite LiDAR signature.
